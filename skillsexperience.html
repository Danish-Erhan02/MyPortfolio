Experiences

Airport Baggage Handler
Sometime after graduation, I took on a job as an airport baggage handler at Changi Airport Terminals 1 & 3. It was a very fulfilling job as I got exposed to the world of baggage handling and what goes on behind the scenes under the terminals. I learnt how baggages are properly tranferred between connecting flights, how missed-flight baggages are handled, how bagage are transferred between terminals and much more. I was intrigued by the logistical workflow of baggages as well as the engineering design of the vast network of conveyor belts. I got to work with a wide range of nationalities such as Malaysians, Burmese and Bangladeshi workers and learnt how to communicate effectively between them. It was also my first time working in a high risk environment. I strongly believe that my experience as a airport baggage handler would give me a taste of the environments and people that I would be working with in the marine and offshore industry.

Final Year Project
My final year project was a group project where we were collaborated with SICK Pte Ltd to build an autonomous vehicle for their new company office. The project titled "Office Transporter" is an autonomous vehicle equipped with a multitude of sensors which transports office documents around to different positions in the office. The industrial grade sensors such as LIDAR, 3D camera and ultrasonic sensors were used for navigation and obstacle avoidance. As the group leader and the mechanical in-charge of the project, my objective was to rebuild the previous iteration of the office transporter to its similar dimensions and make design and mechanical improvements such as improving its ground clearance, designing and testing various suspension systems, optimizing the positions of the sensors and much more. I worked with many different materials and composites and manufacturing techniques such as water jet. I developed a greater understanding of dimensioning and tolerances and how different components of the vehicle fit can seamlessly together. My group was awarded Gold Medal for the MAE FYP exibition and I was awarded a Distinction grade for the project.
